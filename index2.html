Why Book an Aarti?

The Ganga Aarti in Varanasi is not just a ritual; it is a transcendent experience where fire, water, and chanting merge to honor the sacred River Ganga. Booking in advance ensures you witness this spectacle from the best vantage points without the hassle of crowds.

Important Information

Pilgrim Guidelines

1. What is the reporting time for Evening Aarti?

We recommend reaching Dashashwamedh Ghat by 5:30 PM (Winter) or 6:00 PM (Summer) to comfortably take your seats or board your boat. The Aarti usually begins around 6:45 PM.

2. Is photography allowed?

Yes, photography is allowed during the Ganga Aarti. However, flash photography is discouraged near the priests to avoid distraction. Drones are strictly prohibited without police permission.

3. What is the dress code?

There is no strict dress code for viewing the Aarti from the river or steps, but modest attire (covering shoulders and knees) is respectful to the spiritual environment. For temple entry (Mangala Aarti), traditional wear is preferred.

4. Cancellation Policy

Cancellations made 24 hours prior to the booking time are eligible for a 80% refund. Last-minute cancellations are non-refundable.
