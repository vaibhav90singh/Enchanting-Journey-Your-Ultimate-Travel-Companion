Cultural Heritage

Kashi Ke Rang

Varanasi is known as the city of festivals (Barah Mahine Terah Tyohar). Every day is a celebration here, but some days transform the city into a celestial abode. Be it the lighting of a million lamps or the procession of Naga Sadhus, witnessing a festival here is a life-changing experience.

Grand Celebrations

Major Festivals

  • Dev Diwali

    Biggest Event Nov

    The "Diwali of the Gods". All 84 ghats are lit with millions of earthen lamps (Diyas).

  • Maha Shivratri

    Feb/Mar

    The wedding of Lord Shiva and Parvati. A massive procession (Shiv Baraat) moves through the city.

  • Rangbhari Ekadashi

    March

    The day Lord Shiva brings Goddess Parvati to Kashi. Celebrated with Gulal (Colors) at the Temple.

  • Ganga Dussehra

    Summer June

    Celebrates the descent of the Ganga to Earth. Thousands bathe in the river.

  • Rath Yatra

    July

    A grand 3-day fair where Lord Jagannath is taken out in a chariot procession.

  • Durga Puja

    October

    Grand Pandals are set up across the city, ending with immersion in the Ganges.

Unique to Varanasi

Masane Ki Holi

Celebrated at the Manikarnika Ghat (Cremation Ground), this is the most unusual festival in the world. Devotees play Holi not with colors, but with the 'Chita Bhasm' (Ash from pyres), symbolizing that death is not the end, but a part of the divine play of Shiva.

Date: Day after Rangbhari Ekadashi
